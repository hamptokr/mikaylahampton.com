I was born in 1999 in Cincinnati, Ohio. I grew up sandwiched between two brothers, Kramer and Parker. My mother, Christine, and father, Jody, and all of their siblings grew up in “the ‘Nati” as well. My mom and I were outnumbered by the boys, which led them to keep us busy with sports and anything else boys liked to do. Video games, tackle football, climbing trees and making forts outside were normal everyday activities. Aside from power rangers, nerf guns, and backyard baseball, I found two things of my own that I loved that the boys didn’t share the same passion for: soccer and art. I have played soccer since I was three years old. When I wasn’t playing soccer I would sit in my room and draw. My passion for kicking the soccer ball around led me to step off the well-worn path my family had made in Cincinnati and explore a new city. After considernig options near and far, I landed in Evanston, IL where I play for Northwestern University’s Womens Soccer team. My studies at Northwestern include a major in Communication Studies and a Minor within the Segal Design program. I stumbled upon my passion for design in my first year at Northwestern. Thanks to my professors, Pam Daniels and Kim Hoffman, my heart found the creative side of myself that was lost throughout my pursuit of playing soccer at the division 1 level. I had discovered something that didn’t feel like “work” to me. My parents always reiterated the famous quote “do what you love and you’ll never work a day in your life”. Suiting up for the wildcats in the fall and pursuing my design-related interests year round in one of the greatest cities in the world has removed the concept of “work” from my life and brought nothing but great people and experiences into my world.
